Experience

Tata Consultancy Services (for Citibank)

My journey with TCS began with a quiet curiosity for testing — but it quickly evolved into something much more hands-on, technical, and impactful.

Within weeks, I was contributing to Citibank’s internal engineering workflows, surrounded by production-grade systems and high-stakes release cycles. The environment demanded precision, consistency, and the kind of ownership that goes beyond just executing test cases.

I focused on designing test frameworks that scaled, not just worked. Using Selenium, Cucumber, and TestNG, I streamlined regression processes to catch failures early — before users ever noticed them. On the backend, I crafted REST-assured suites to validate hundreds of APIs, ensuring stability across service integrations, authentication layers, and data flows.

What started as test automation became a deeper practice in engineering quality: contributing to CI/CD pipelines, aligning with sprint cycles, writing SQL for test data accuracy, and collaborating directly with developers, analysts, and UAT teams to push each build closer to confidence. Documentation, test architecture, defect traceability — everything mattered. And I made sure it was done right.

Those three years taught me that good QA isn't just about testing. It's about building systems users can trust — and being the first person who tries to break them before they do.

QSpiders, Chennai

Before the real-world systems came in, this was my training ground. I spent time refining automation principles, learning Selenium through repetition, and debugging test failures until they made sense.

The focus was on consistency, logic, and getting comfortable with structured testing in live environments — a foundation I still carry forward.

Shiash Info Solutions, Chennai

My first encounter with a working product. At Shiash, I observed how software really behaves under test — how APIs connect to interfaces, how small bugs create ripple effects, and why functional coverage matters.

It was less about tools, more about instincts. And it sparked my need to understand systems from the inside out.
